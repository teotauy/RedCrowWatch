Privacy Policy

Red Crow Labs — RedCrowWatch — Last updated April 2026

Overview

RedCrowWatch is a traffic violation monitoring research project operated by Red Crow Labs. This policy explains how we handle data collected or observed by the system.

What We Monitor

The RedCrowWatch system uses computer vision to observe public street activity at designated intersections. The system detects and classifies:

  • Vehicle types and dimensions (to identify oversized commercial vehicles)
  • Traffic signal compliance
  • Pedestrian crossing patterns
  • Audible events (emergency sirens, horn honks)

What We Do Not Collect

RedCrowWatch does not collect, store, or transmit:

  • Facial images or biometric data of any kind
  • License plate numbers or vehicle identification
  • Personal information about individuals observed
  • Location data tied to specific persons

All detection is event-based and anonymized. No video footage is stored or shared. The system records only violation type, zone, and timestamp.

Dashboard Data

This dashboard currently displays sample/simulated data for demonstration purposes. When live monitoring is active, only aggregated, anonymized violation counts are displayed. No personally identifiable information is ever shown.

Cookies and Analytics

This website does not use cookies or third-party analytics. No tracking scripts are loaded. No data about dashboard visitors is collected or stored.

Data Retention

Violation event records (type, zone, timestamp only) are retained for a rolling 90-day window for analytical purposes. No raw video or image data is retained.

Public Space Notice

This system operates in public spaces where individuals have no reasonable expectation of privacy. Monitoring is conducted solely for traffic safety research and civic advocacy purposes, consistent with applicable New York State and City laws.
